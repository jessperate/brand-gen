Web Project Process

Web requests have their own tiering system based on design and dev complexity. Use this to understand the level of process required before submitting.

Tier 1
Complex build
Use case hub, archetype quiz, blog redesign, resource center, interactive tools, campaign microsites, new product or solution pages
Qualifies if: net-new URL requiring significant design + dev work, new CMS collection or architecture, custom interactions or integrations, or a major structural redesign of a high-traffic page.

High-strategy, high-design-complexity builds. These require a full brief, cross-functional review, and sign-off from design, dev, and marketing before any work begins.

Start with a Tier 1 project brief in Notion and align with Jess before anything moves to Asana. No exceptions -- skipping the brief adds weeks, not days.

Part 03

What every request needs

Request type + size
Know what you're asking for before you submit. "Can you make something for the webinar?" is not a brief.
Final copy
All headline, body copy, CTAs, speaker names, and dates confirmed. Draft copy = paused project.
Asset specs
Exact dimensions, format (PNG/SVG/Webflow), and destination (social, email, paid, web). Assume nothing.
Reference links or files
Headshots, logos, example pages, Figma links, Notion briefs -- attach them at submission, not after.
Hard deadline with context
"ASAP" is not a deadline. Tell us the publish date and why it's fixed -- that helps us prioritize accurately.
One DRI per request
Every request needs a single person who owns feedback and approvals. Multiple stakeholders with contradictory feedback will be resolved by the DRI, not the design team.

Part 05

How this works in practice

Do this
+Submit via the #creative-requests form with all assets attached upfront
+Include the publish/go-live date so we understand the actual constraint
+Lock copy before submitting -- especially for landing pages
+Batch related assets into one request when possible
+Ping Jess for Tier 3 work before formally submitting to align on capacity
+Give one round of consolidated feedback, not drip edits over days
Don't do this
–DM the designers directly -- requests without a ticket don't get tracked or prioritized
–Submit with "ASAP" as a deadline -- we need a real date
–Submit with placeholder or draft copy and expect design to start
–Ask for same-day or next-day turnaround on Tier 2–3 work
–Change scope mid-ticket -- new ask = new ticket
–Submit without an Asana task for anything Tier 2 or above
Part 06

Recurring & standing needs

Some request types come up every week or every launch cycle. Templates exist for these -- use them.

Webinar graphics
Template exists for 1, 2, and 3 speakers. Square + landscape + vertical. Submit speaker headshots + bios with the request, not after.
Customer story assets
Standard set: hero image, 2 quote cards, 1 diagram (before/after or workflow). Jordan owns these -- route via Jordan first.
Paid social creative batches
Jim submits these in batches (2 concepts per 2-week sprint). That cadence is the right model -- avoid one-off ad requests unless urgent.
Email templates
HubSpot modules exist for product launches, webinar promos, reports, events, and asset distribution. Check with Julia before requesting a net-new template.
Webinar landing pages
Templated in Webflow -- 1, 2, and 3 speaker variants exist. Need HubSpot form? Account for that at submission; retrofitting adds a day.
